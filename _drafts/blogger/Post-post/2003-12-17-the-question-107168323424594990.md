"What do you love when you love your God?"
<br />
<br />The beauty of this question is that it doesn't presuppose <em>what</em> God loves.  Instead it places the question soley on the believer and her/his perspective.
<br />
<br />I simplfy the question as:  "what are we <em>really</em> loving?"  Are we in love with the God-who-is-there (commonly characterized as: omniscient, omnipresent, wholly other) or are we actually in love with loving the concept of God?  Can we label it "wrong" (or "not sufficient") to love loving God?  Can we love God if God is wholly other?  Am I priviledged to even use the word "we"?
