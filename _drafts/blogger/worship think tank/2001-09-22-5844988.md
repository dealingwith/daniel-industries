Hi all,
<br />
<br />All the discussion about the reaction/response for the attacks has had us mentioning grace alot.  Now that is topic I know very little about in a tangible way.  How do we embody grace in a world that relishes hate.  Each time I hear about something that seems to project grace I doubt it, too much cultural programming I guess.  I do have many experiences of grace though.  Our two younest kids Isaiah and Micah were abandoned in J'burg South Africa.  Isaiah was found in a dumpster, which probably saved his life becuase it was very cold when they found him.  Micah was left in a park and most likely found quickly.  For me the fact that each of these boys who may have most likely died have been given a life.  By coming to be part of our family, the people who found them, the people who cared for them in SA it is all examples of embodied grace.  If we allow ourselves to be open to the Spirit and act in passion and boldness I believe we can embody grace.  All we can control about our life is how we react and act towards others and their actions.  I think when I ponder the last days I need to be more open to grace than I let myself.  Not sure this is going  anywhere but what do y'all think.

<br />John
