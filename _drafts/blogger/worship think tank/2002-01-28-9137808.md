<br /><a href="http://www.greenbelt.org.uk/insight/">Inight Home</a> Joan Jeanrenaud, formerly of the Kronos Quartet, is doing a short tour in England during February & March with what looks like an interesting programme involving video art as well as music. There seems to be a certain strain of religious thought running through the pieces.
<br />
<br />"Throughout the performance, Joan explores new ways of creating and performing music, inspired by visions of the future and emotions of the past. Mark Grey's 'Blood Red' uses her Zeta electric cello linked to realtime computer sampling, while her own work 'Altarpiece' was written in response to an array of altars seen on the Day of the Dead. 
<br />
<br />Metamorphosis also celebrates long time associations and friendships. At the core of the evening are sections of Philip Glass' intense, romantic, masterpiece 'Metamorphosis' illuminated by three video artists who create a stunning triptych of images linked to Joan's performance. Joan draws particular inspiration for her work from the Fluxus art movement, represented by Yoko Ono's 'Lighting Piece' and 'Wood Piece'. These works inform the new visual counterpoint created in works by Karen Tanaka ('The Song of Songs') and Steve Mackey ('Cairn'), and a new arrangement of Hamza El Din's gorgeous cycle 'Escalay' (Waterwheel) which she first played on the classic Kronos album 'Pieces of Africa' "
