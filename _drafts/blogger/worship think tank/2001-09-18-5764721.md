Daniel,
<br />
<br />I agree with the statement that we can never know God, I think at its essence God is forever unknowable.  The only way we know anything of God is his/her actions towards. with and around us.  For me that is what the Bible is about.  I do think that this discussion is pointless at some level, because we will never know the answer.  Rudolf Otto, "The Idea of the Holy" wrote a little book about this unknowability that you speak of, 
<br />
<br />I think a big part of my struggle with this whole omni issue is what it says about us, our inablility to be comfortable with the unknown and the mystery of God.  I know God is and that I will never leave, but for me the pursuit of God has to be in intellectual and praxis.  I think that is why I and my family have made many of the choices we have, adoption, seminary, etc.  Modernity killed mystery and we must rediscover it or we too will perish as a witness to our culture.  Unless we can use our intellectual ponderings with action we will be what Paul called a resounding gong, an empty voice in a cacophany of voices.  I quess we are all to blame for what happened and if we are maybe God is too.
