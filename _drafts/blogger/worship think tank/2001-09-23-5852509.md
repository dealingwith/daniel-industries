Hi all,
<br />
<br />Just wanted to share something with you.  We have heard  much about how kids have processed all this death and destruction.  I was just praying with my three year old and his pray was that all the people who died would be made alive again so their families would not have to miss them anymore.  It struck me how my little boy is so open to God that he thinks if he asks all those people will return to their families.  I know it will never happen in this world, but as Creed says "But, my friend, I'd sacrifice all those nights If I could make the Earth and my dreams the same"  Someday I will realize that peace. power, release of pain, unending worship and reveling in God's presence.  Noah's words made my spirit soar as I thought about what is to come.  I know it seems hopeless in the midst of this chaos, but we are given glimpses of what is to come.  The deja vu of heaven, something we already know deep in our spirits.  It is that hope that sustains me in times of despair and pain.  Yet, I relish the fight here and the small victories achieved like the innonce of my son's heart.  May God bless us all as we struggle and live and breath and have our being in this momentary place that is not our home.
<br />
<br />Blessings
<br />John
