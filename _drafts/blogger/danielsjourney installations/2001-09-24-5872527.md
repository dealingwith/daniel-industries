Here is the introduction to "hate mail," for those coming at this ahead of time. If you don't want to spoil any part of the installation, then read no further of this post!
<br />
<br />"Abuse" isn't the right word, but it's the first one that comes to mind.
<br />See Also: Confusion
<br />See Also: Horror
<br />See Also: Tears
<br />See Also: Hate 
<br />
<br />This work began as a response to the first hate mail Miriam or I have ever received. I'm sure it will not be the last, however as the first it had a great affect on us. 
<br />
<br />Three days later, on September 11 2001, the most terrible act of my lifetime occurred in New York City and Washington D.C. 
<br />
<br />Without knowing how to respond to these acts of abuse and hatred on both a micro and a macro scale, I immediately searched for meaning. 
<br />
<br />I'm convinced there is none. 
<br />
<br />This work, therefore, is about that struggle to find meaning in the face of both small and inconceivably large pain. 
<br />
<br />There is no universal meaning for abuse, hate, terrorism, bigotry. There are only small connections we can make each day that keep scraps of sanity in our cupboards of life. 
<br />
<br />This work is about my attempt at a few small connections
