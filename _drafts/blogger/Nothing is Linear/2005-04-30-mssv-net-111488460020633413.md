<a href="http://mssv.net/">mssv.net</a>, "massively multiuser online entertainment · biology · space"<blockquote>...is the personal weblog of Adrian Hon, featuring articles on massively<br />multiplayer online entertainment and science. The 'middling' and 'tiny'<br />sections include more general posts and links.<br /><br />When someone asks me what I do, I used to be able to say that I was<br />a neuroscientist. This was a wonderfully simple and, as it turned out,<br />apparently very impressive answer, especially when backed up with the<br />whole Oxford-PhD thing.<br /><br />Unfortunately, those days are now gone. I left my PhD at Oxford in August 2004 to work in London on something called <a href="http://www.perplexcity.com">Perplex City</a><br />(formerly named Project Syzygy). Gone was the easy answer to that<br />standard question. For the first few weeks in my new job, I actually<br />attempted to give an accurate answer, which went something along the<br />lines of:<br /><br />"Well, do you remember the movie A.I. by Steven Speilberg? See, Microsoft and Dreamworks SKG did <a href="http://www.cloudmakers.org">this marketing campaign</a> for the film..." and so on.<br /><br />This wasn't an answer that could last. Not only was it too long, but<br />people still didn't understand what it was that I did. So I changed it<br />to:<br /><br />"I work in a multimedia entertainment company developing a new type of cross-platform game."<br /><br />Slightly better, but not really any more informative since I<br />generally ended up explaining the whole thing all over again. I then<br />made a radical change to it and now when asked, I simply say:<br /><br />"I'm a puzzle designer."<br /><br />If they want to know more, fine, if not, at least we've both saved<br />some time. It's not a particularly accurate answer but it'll have to do<br />until Perplex City launches and I can just tell people that I work on<br />that.<br /><br />I do other things apart from being a 'puzzle designer'. I'm the editor of <a href="http://www.newmars.com">New Mars</a><br /><br />(which I have been horribly neglecting of late) and a member of the<br />Mars Society UK steering committee, and I hold several other positions<br />within the Mars Society International. In 2001, I was a moderator for<br />the <a href="http://www.cloudmakers.org">Cloudmakers</a> community following Microsoft's AI online immersive game and wrote the <a href="http://www.vavatch.co.uk/guide">Guide</a> for the game.<br /><br />I still retain a healthy interest in neuroscience and recently I<br />contributed a chapter to 'Mind Hacks', a neuroscience book being<br />published by O'Reilly.</blockquote><p>Most recently with the very interesting <a href="http://www.mssv.net/archives/000665.shtml">The Reality Artificers</a>: "How the BBC, Orson Welles, ancient Egyptian scribes and alternate reality game designers all follow the same 3900 year old tradition."</p>
