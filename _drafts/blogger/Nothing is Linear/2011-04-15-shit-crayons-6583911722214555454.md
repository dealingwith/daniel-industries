<a href="http://www.bogost.com/writing/shit_crayons.shtml">Ian Bogost makes a scathing critique of social games</a>:<blockquote>Several years ago, Chaim Gingold gave us the useful concept of the Magic Crayon. A magic crayon is a tool that facilitates creativity in a way that wouldn't otherwise be possible. A magic crayon lets its users breathe life into things.<br />
<br />
Some would like to think that all crayons are magic ones. That just any old thing can conjure. But that's not true. The magic crayon has a shadow side.<br />
<br />
Some barriers are benign, but others are insidious...<br />
<br />
Inspirations like that are not magic crayons, but shit crayons...<br />
<br />
Even if creativity comes from constraint, there's constraint and there's incarceration. A despot in a sorcerer's hat does not deserve praise for inciting desperate resilience.</blockquote>
