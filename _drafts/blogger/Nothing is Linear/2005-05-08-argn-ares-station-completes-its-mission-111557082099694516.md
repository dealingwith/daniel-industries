<a href="http://www.argn.com/archive/000258ares_station_completes_its_mission.php">ARGN: Ares Station Completes Its Mission</a><blockquote><img alt="aresstation.jpg" src="http://danielsjourney.com/blog/files/2005/05/ares.png" align="left" border="0" height="157" style="margin:10px;" width="100" />The indie Alternate Reality Game <a href="http://www.argn.com/archive/000215ares_station_launches.php">Ares Station</a> came to a close on May 5th, with an announcement on its <a href="http://www.aresstation.com/">trailhead page</a>. Although nobody likes it when a game ends, those who played the two-month long ARG seemed to be extremely happy with the game overall.<p>Ares Station was designed by <a href="http://www.iub.edu/">Indiana University Bloomington</a> graduate students Nathan Mishler and Will Emigh as a Telecommunications thesis project. Ares Station represents about a year of work, and included excellent quality design and multimedia elements, as well as a very effective "stealth" launch.</p></blockquote>
