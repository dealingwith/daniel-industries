<small>(repost, now with more video)<br /><br /><strong><a href="#sxswupdates">Updates</a></strong></small><br /><br />SXSW inspired a lot of cognitive dissonance. I'm not even sure I can get it all out into words. It's clear in my brain. I don't have a lot of time for really cohesive blogging anymore, but I'll try.<br /><br />Part of that dissonance might be related to that last thing. There were a <em>lot</em> people at SXSW with <em>plenty</em> of time to <em>blog</em> about a <em>lot</em> of things and have <em>very important</em> thoughts. And by "very important" I mean asinine and pathetic. <a href="http://search.twitter.com/search?q=+d-bag+OR+douchebag+OR+douchebags+OR+bullshit+OR+blog+OR+blogger+from%3Adealingwith" title="I wasn&#39;t being facetious about the &#39;I like how starting a blog is the new...&#39; thing, though">That combined with a huge emphasis on panels and "core conversations" inspired a lot of hate</a>.<br /><br /><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/armiller/3362592775/"><img src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3572/3362592775_9e2ec81c1b_m.jpg" style="float:right; margin:10px; clear:both;" /></a>At the same time, I was again <em>really</em> inspired by SXSW this year. If there was one overriding theme to the entire event, it was this: <strong>Things suck, but you're awesome</strong>. Most presentations (and yes I tried to stick to the proper presentations) ended with some variant of <a href="http://images.google.com/images?q=your+awesome+who%27s&amp;btnG=Search+Images">this</a> (and <a href="http://twitter.com/KathySierra" title="god bless her, I have no idea why she picked those colors for her Twitter page. to drive away stalkers, perhaps?">Kathy Sierra</a>'s really did).<br /><br /><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/carissabyers/3354053054/"><img src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3471/3354053054_025d2eddee_m.jpg" style="float:right; margin:10px; clear:both;" /></a>Saturday I mostly played in the Lego pit and hit up <a href="http://twitter.com/barcampaustin">BarCamp</a>, but I did make it to <a href="http://tinyurl.com/co8n43">Designing the Future of The New York Times</a>, which I went to, of course, because of the brilliant <a href="http://www.subtraction.com/">Khoi Vinh</a>, but found myself far more impressed with his colleague <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Bodkin">Tom Bodkin</a> (<a href="http://sxsw.com/interactive/talks/schedule?action=bio&amp;id=196207">here's his bio w/ pic on the SX site</a>). His years of experience in a much richer design process and culture spoke rather profoundly, I thought, to a room full of mostly design hacks (myself, of course, included).<br /><br /><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/steventrotter/3358353079/"><img src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3457/3358353079_d0a817851c_m.jpg" style="float:right; margin:10px; clear:both;" /></a>My first two <s>panels</s> talks on Sunday were <em>excellent</em>. First was <a href="http://sxsw.com/interactive/talks/schedule?action=show&amp;id=IAP0900124">Being a UX Team of One</a> by <a href="http://www.adaptivepath.com/aboutus/leah.php">Leah Buley</a>, which was totally humbling and inspiring at the same time. Lots of great stuff that I <em>want</em> to do but am <em>not</em> doing yet in my UX practice. <a href="http://www.slideshare.net/ugleah">She has a few preso's up already on slideshare</a>, including this same one given last year. The second was <a href="http://sxsw.com/interactive/talks/schedule?action=show&amp;id=IAP0901366">Jared Spool's talk</a>. I've seen Jared speak before so I knew it was going to be good. It was. Apparently other people knew it was going to be good too. About two rows in front of me was a who's who of web design.<br /><br />Somehow I missed <a href="http://sxsw.com/interactive/talks/schedule?action=show&amp;id=IAP0901148">this</a> on Sunday, which was dumb of me. After giving the keynote a miss to go visit <a href="http://www.mellowjohnnys.com/" title="Lance Armstrong&#39;s new bike shop">Mellow Johnny's</a> with <a href="http://twitter.com/Quadmod">Jay</a> I wandering in and out of sessions until stumbling into <a href="http://sxsw.com/interactive/talks/schedule?action=show&amp;id=IAP0900377">Gary Vaynerchuk</a>'s Q&A in the big room. I was so glad I did. My opinion of him did a 180. Previously, I wasn't a fan (go <a href="http://garyvaynerchuk.com/">here</a> to decide for yourself). But seeing him speak live to that audience I realized, <em>he's a motivational speaker</em>, and my kind of motivational speaker at that: a no bullshit one. Plus he could get Eeyore excited about life.<br /><br /><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/debbieweil/3362991934/"><img src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3442/3362991934_e4c64a3595_m.jpg" style="float:right; margin:10px; clear:both;" /></a>Monday there was some more good stuff going on, but all I really cared about was the <a href="http://sxsw.com/interactive/talks/schedule?action=show&amp;id=IAP0901379">Bruce Sterling session</a>. I did go to <a href="http://sxsw.com/interactive/talks/schedule?action=show&amp;id=IAP0900297">Presenting Straight to the Brain</a>, which was good, Kathy's stuff particularly. I have strong opinions about that stuff, though, and I can't believe no-one is poo-pooing panels and telling people to emulate rockstars. Mid-day I got to hang out with @<a href="http://twitter.com/thedandee">thedandee</a> some more which was a good thing. Eating, as well, since I hadn't eaten in about 24 hours. After lunch we both went to the aforementioned and inspiring <a href="http://sxsw.com/interactive/talks/schedule?action=show&amp;id=IAP0901322">talk</a> by Kathy. Then, Bruce. He was awesome.<br /><br />I'll be <a href="http://huffduffer.com/dealingwith">huffduffing</a> all <a href="http://huffduffer.com/dealingwith/tags/sxsw">the talks I went to plus all the ones I wished I'd gone to</a>, for those who want to catch/keep up but want a filter.
