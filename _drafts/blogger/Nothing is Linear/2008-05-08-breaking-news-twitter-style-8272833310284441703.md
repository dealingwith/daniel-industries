<blockquote>News of a possible explosion rippled through the popular online service <a href="http://twitter.com/home">Twitter</a> on Tuesday, in a preview of what’s to come in the realm of breaking news and citizen journalism. Twitter is a so-called microblogging site that allows users to send and receive short messages.<br /><br />At about 1:37 pm, software developer <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_Winer">Dave Winer</a> asked the Twitterverse: "<a href="http://twitter.com/davewiner/statuses/804852522">Explosion in Falls Church, VA?</a>"</blockquote><a href="http://blogs.reuters.com/mediafile/2008/05/06/breaking-news-twitter-style/">Breaking news, Twitter style</a><br /><br />What is so interesting about Twitter is what it is further exposing about what the web has been showing us for the last 15 years, that is a social experience of hyperconnectedness, attention, and most of all, mediation.
