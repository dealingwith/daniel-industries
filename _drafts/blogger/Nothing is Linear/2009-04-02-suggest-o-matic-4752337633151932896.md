<p>I'm typing this in <a href="http://danielsjourney.com/suggestomatic/">Suggest-o-matic</a> (SOM), the very simplified culmination of many years of thinking about content management, or what I refer to as "creative management", that is, how we store and reference all the content we create over the course of a life.</p><p><img src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2181/2163451008_196e4f79f0_m.jpg" style="margin: 5px; float: right;" title="Not a reference librarian, but aesthetically, it was right" />Right now, SOM is mostly like an automated reference librarian, who <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CvZt3t2z_w">looks over your shoulder</a> as you're writing your paper and suggests things out on the interwebs that might have to do with what you're writing about.</p><p><a href="http://danielsjourney.com/suggestomatic/">This</a> is its very first iteration, what I am calling a <a href="http://danielsjourney.com/suggestomatic/">0.1 protoype</a> ...my intentions for the future are:</p><ul> <li>create multiple links for any given query (right now it can only automatically create one link based on what you've clipped from your query)</li><br /> <li>improving on the UI</li><br /> <li>ability to favor any number of site searches (I'm most interested in searching <em>my</em> old content for articles, images, etc. that I might have already created on the subjects)</li><br /> <li>an automatic post-to-my-blog-or-whatever dealio</li><br /> <li>a run-on-any-page, white box + selected text Suggest-o-matic</li><br /> <li>serious natural language chops, so that special syntax is no longer needed to fire off a suggestion</li><br /> <li>serious AI on results, so that SOM can sort and manage the relative links automatically for you</li></ul><strong>Update: Need I mention that IE is completely unsupported at this point!?</strong>
