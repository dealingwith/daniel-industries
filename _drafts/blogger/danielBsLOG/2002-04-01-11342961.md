"We are taught in countless ways to value the product, the 
<br />prize, the climactic moment. But even after we've just 
<br />caught the winning pass in the Superbowl, there's always 
<br />tomorrow and tomorrow and tomorrow. If our life is a good 
<br />one, a life of mastery, most of it will be spent on the 
<br />plateau.... Where in our upbringing, our schooling, our 
<br />career are we explicitly taught to value, to enjoy, even 
<br />to love the plateau, the long stretch of diligent effort 
<br />with no seeming progress?"
<br />
<br />From FedEx to Who Wants To Be a Millionaire?, we're over- 
<br />conditioned to want great results overnight. But all who have 
<br />truly mastered anything know that the formula for MASTERY is 
<br />long stretches of diligent effort, broken up once in a big 
<br />while by "pops" to the next level. Since that's the case, we 
<br />oughta take joy in our rehearsals -- after all, that's where 
<br />the greatest will spend the majority of their time.
<br />
