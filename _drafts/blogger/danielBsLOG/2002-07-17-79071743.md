from <a href="../flight_chap3.html">chapter 3</a> of my own <a href="../flight.html">the flight</a>:
<br />
<br />...anyone who hasn't seriously considered suicide has simply just not fully considered the reality of his or her current situation. If one thinks long enough about where they are in the scheme of things, that is things in the sense of what the hell we are doing amongst all these people, all these living beings, all these created things, all these quiet creatures of the soil, all this air floating about us, all these waves traveling around and through us, all that space and all those planets and all that great unknown, all those possible alternatives that exist right beside yet fully apart from us…then they can fully recognize the reality of their life. That it is of no consequence and they should donate their organs before they expire from some more natural or careful means. 
<br />
<br />So what keeps so many of us from doing ourselves in and forgetting about the day-to-day torment? What allows some to stop fighting it? I wish I knew...
