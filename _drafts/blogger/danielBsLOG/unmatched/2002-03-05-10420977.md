When David Sarno moved to a new apartment on the Upper West Side of Manhattan recently, he learned he would have to wait several weeks for the phone company to install a fast Internet connection. But after opening his laptop, he discovered with a surge of delight that he was already able to check his e-mail and call up Web sites at lightning-fast speeds.
<br />
<br />Someone nearby had Wi-Fi, the technology behind the short-range, inexpensive and often unsecured wireless networks that are rapidly sprinkling the nation with sweet spots of airborne high-speed Internet access.
