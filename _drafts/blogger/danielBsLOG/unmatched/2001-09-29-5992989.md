It's 8:05 on a Saturday morning and I'm up and alone. Miriam went this morning in the pouring rain to take the foreign service exam. It would be so great to see that dream of hers come true--to work in foreign countries; but what else could that mean? with embassies having been bombed in Africa and all the other turbulence around the world right now. How such events can shake an already shakey boat.
<br />
<br />We might actually sell the house. One less thing in the long term turns into a big thing in the short term, but it would save us a lot every month and give us the freedom to up and move at any time.
<br />
<br />My hand already hurts from typing. I typed alot yesterday and this keyboard isn't the best. I'm working on two computers and the one with the better keyboard is just too slow for this kind of thing (web apps).
<br />
<br />Anyway, how strange to be waving goodbye to Miriam at 7 o'clock in the morning in the pouring rain. A short prayer for mercy from God and then she was gone for the day. It's so dark out still. I can't believe it's 8 o'clock. Looks like it's going to be a very dark, very wet day. Thank God for a roof. May it hold up under this torrent.
