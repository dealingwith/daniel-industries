Been thinking a lot about art lately. Perhaps it's because of <a href="http://integration.danielsjourney.com">integration research</a>, but who really knows.
<br />
<br /><img src="http://www.vonnegut.com/images/art/joepetro.jpg" align="left">I keep discovering artists as of late, again I don't know why. I honestly didn't know who <a href="http://www.vonnegut.com/">Vonnegut</a> was two weeks ago, and now I've seen his name pop up everywhere, it seems. Same with <a href="http://www.centralbooking.com/author_about.shtml?author=55">Tom Robbins</a>, whose book now graces my bookshelf, and there are more coming in the mail.
<br />
<br />Another thing that has intriqued me is the multi-valenced --and I must aside here, because I stole that from Ryan, who flattered me by describing my work that way after my performance on Friday...Miriam <a href="http://www.danielsjourney.com/miriam/2002_09_01_archives.html#81918631">mentioned his email to us</a> which was also very encouraging-- nature of these artists. I mentioned one of my favorite artists <a href="http://www.michaelknott.com/">Michael Knott</a> last week (and covered one of his songs Friday). He has been doing music, painting, and now sculpture. 1giantleap (see link from prior post) is a couple of musicians with digital video cameras and and eye for "traveling right" (as I would say).
<br />
<br />The main thread I can see in all of these <i>human beings</i> is that they are living what I would call an <i>artistic life</i>. Perhaps this is what I am after with my tagline <i>seeking :: stories :: beauty :: meaning</i> (recently appended with "whatever the fuck <i>that</i> means" at live performances).
<br />
<br />To wit (not sure what that means exactly but I like it): Yesterday I penned this to a friend in the UK:
<br />
<br />i have a theory that god raises all the really great musicians for himself and kills them early so he can have them to himself (at the time i was talking about jeff buckley). i am facinated by artists such as pollock, who had such genius, yet such insecurity and such destructive tendencies. sometimes i wonder if they were just too close to god's creativity to cope down here on earth. then i wonder if that's just a great excuse for my own insecure masicism. still, it's a shame most great artists are broke and hated during all or most of their lives, and only after they're gone do we really begin to appreciate what they did and said. this i do know for sure: it's because they are the prophets, and the better they are, the further off the realization of that prophecy is. many times it is realized after their death. so if follows no one is interested. to be a real lover of art you have to either have a prophetic eye yourself or just blind faith in the prophet. it's sad, really, that in a fear of the prophets the modern church has forced itself into irrelevancy.

<br />And in a final bit of noise, speaking of Buckley, about a week ago I <i>finally</i> got the Jeff Buckley DVD. Buckley has been one of those artists for me that has taken a long time to warm up to. But those are always the best. They don't wear out quickly. Considering it has taken years for me to get into him, I don't think he'll <i>ever</i> wear. This DVD is amazing. I'm going to try and get some of the audio from the EPK (Electronic Press Kit for you non-music-industry-types) onto some highly-compressed mp3 for you all so, if you haven't already, you can hear this amazing prophet softly speak the Truth to you.
