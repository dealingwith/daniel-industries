<font color=#336699>When I called brother, he was understandably unamused. 
<br />
<br />“What the fuck are you thinking?” I think were his exact words.
<br />
<br />My brother never minces words.
<br />
<br />He then went on to go off about how stupid it was for me to leave home in the first place, going to New York to be poor and deformed, taken advantage of, and desperate; all of which culminated with the scheme I had just described to him, imploring him to help me by participating.
<br />
<br />You see, Rob required brothers.
<br />
<br />I told him in no uncertain terms which part I would play, and the benefits he would gain, the treatment he would receive. 
<br />
<br />He told me I was crazy.
<br />
<br />I told him this was my calling. I told him I would do nothing else with my life but this. I told him he would either help me be in this play or I would kill myself.
<br />
<br />He told me I had become one of those New York fatalists. All the terror threats had gone to my head. I was too ready to bet it all.
<br />
<br />I told him it was my destiny. I told him I would be famous. I told him he would be rich.
<br />
<br />He mumbled something.
<br />
<br />I said nothing.
<br />
<br />He said “Ok. What the hell. If that’s what you want. I can’t stop you.”
<br />
<br />I said “Great.
<br />
<br />“Now, we need to make plans. You need to be here for the audition. This is the only thing you’ll have to pay for, I promise.”
<br />
<br />“Ok. No problem. I’ll be there in two days.”
