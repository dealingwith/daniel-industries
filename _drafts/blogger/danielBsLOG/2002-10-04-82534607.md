<b>Church Youth Rush to Eat Their Veggies</b> -- <i><a href="http://www.reuters.com/">Reuters</a> -- Chicago</i> -- October 4, 2002 -- Churches all across the United States have made extensive preparations for the opening of the Veggie Tales movie, <a href="http://www.jonahmovie.com/">Jonah</a>, tonight. In addition to the large number of white middle-class families expected at the premier, there is also a very large teen and pre-teen contingent to watch out for, says national youth worker and English teacher <a href="http://monkhouse.blogspot.com/">David Hopkins</a>. "These 'youth group kids,' as those of us in the industry call them, can not only cause social pandemonium in public spaces," Hopkins said in an interview earlier today, "but their acceptance or rejection of the Jonah movie will most certainly single-handedly spell the financial rise or ruin of BigIdea." <a href="http://www.bigidea.com/">BigIdea</a>, the Lombard, IL, based media company responsible for veggie-mania, is indeed banking on the success of Jonah. "I haven't felt this much stress and pressure to succeed since I worked for the Southern Baptist Convention," said Phil Vischer, founder of BigIdea and originator of all BigIdea's big ideas. 
<br />
<br /><img src="http://www.oklahoma.net/~silvrdal/boblarry.gif" align="left" hspace="10px">However, if the ramp up of church youth groups throughout the 48 continental states is any indication, Vischer will be able to sleep tight this weekend. "There's never been an event this big, ever…in my experience, at least," said <a href="mailto:father_cum_mings@christianpornmachine.com">Father Cummings</a>, a youth worker in the Catholic church in Boston, MA. In more than 5,000 churches, youth pastors and volunteers (mostly community college students and retirees) set up tables, tea, and tracks in their church facilities and in front of movie theaters. One church in suburban Oklahoma City, OK, actually printed "Veggies for your mind and soul" tracks to hand out during the premier tonight. Church leaders, who preferred to remain nameless, reported that they originally planned on using "Jonah got his warning, here's yours" tracks, tying in with the theme of the movie, but upon consultation with <a href="http://www.leonardsweet.com/">Leonard Sweet</a>, a famous church writer and coffee shop commenter, they decided against it with a 5-4 elder board vote.

<br />Across the border in Canada, even though the total population is only 350, church leaders have realized both the spiritual and financial implications of Jonah. "With so many church-going teenagers amassing in one place (the Jonah premier tonight --<i>Ed.</i>), this is a tremendous opportunity for the church to save these kids--some of them for the third or fourth time," said <a href="http://www.jordoncooper.sk.ca/">Jordon Cooper</a>, a pastor at <a href="http://www.lakeviewchurch.com/">Lakeview Church</a> in <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/maps/print.adp?mapdata=hP0urGiog%2be1PIOjKvDnODG%2f7Emb2XEjJz1%2f%2fZ0hFrgJX%2bsS%2f%2f6YzWIIpuFOHJ%2b%2fP6ToLHQkdk65wpBt0gx4bRMtiIX9%2fnByHEREkOsWsdz4vPP8wte5Ca5ZdrYd%2be%2f7HO%2bjfZipkOIFQPcyZS81vFZtgJkbWe9hrCrFJnA0XQQIgTalVysW89hNODxNXfbg1SGZdGdlYa7YnO6zcGunN%2bKu7id4FHzzQdwhfyyCElnf25xnLdCoi9RXQSJT5BRJMPlm6ZJrOixaIZulXoSICaqWUjQ89nFWZFn199pJ6%2f0%3d">Saskatoon</a>, Saskatchewan. "Besides, if these youth group kids don't make it out and BigIdea goes under, we'll be back to <a href="http://www.bibleman.com/">BibleMan</a>, and no one wants that, really."
