Language training
<br />
<br />You might have noticed I've been posting prior to 2 pm this week. That's because I dropped my 5 hour Bosnian class and traded it in for a 1 hour morning sit-in with Miriam. She's now taking her admin training from 9-4.
<br />
<br />The thing was, I had 5 hours a day to work on Bosnian, but that was <i>all</i> the time I had for it. And to get it one really needs to study an additional 3-4 hours on top of that. And even if I had the time, after 5 hours my brain was fried and all I was good for was staring blankly at one of my many projects. 
<br />
<br />You might have <a href="http://www.danielsjourney.com/index.php?archive=2002_09_01_archive.xml&id_pass=81694548">seen</a> <a href="http://www.danielsjourney.com/index.php?archive=2002_09_01_archive.xml&id_pass=81840449">this</a> <a href="http://www.danielsjourney.com/index.php?archive=2002_09_01_archive.xml&id_pass=81745767">coming….</a> <a href="http://www.danielsjourney.com/index.php?archive=2002_09_01_archive.xml&id_pass=81729997">etc.</a>
<br />
<br />There are many factors, a new teacher last week who seemed to hate me, a book which is complete shite but cannot be changed because it was dictated by the head of the department, and a language that is <i>so f-ing complicated</i>! Every sentence involves about 10 calculations.
<br />
<br /><i>I eat old cheese and drink cold beer.</i>
<br /><s>accusative verb / masculine inanimate object / accusative / neuter object
<br /><i>Ja jed<b>em</b> star<b>i</b> sir i pij<b>em</b> hdlad<b>ne</b> piv<b>o</b>.</i>
<br />
<br />I <i>think</i>. Most likely tho, I'm wrong.</s>
<br />
<br /><b>Update:</b> Miriam came home and helped me out: 
<br />first person singular personal pronoun / first person singular verb / accusative case masculine singular adjective / accusative masculine inanimate singular object / accusative neuter singular adjective / accusative neuter singular inanimate object
<br /><i>Ja jed<b>em</b> star<b>i</b> sir i pij<b>em</b> hlad<b>no</b> piv<b>o</b>.</i>
<br />
<br />I only misspelled "cold" is all. :)
<br />
<br />When we learn languages growing up, first of all, we have years and years to learn it. Then we learn to speak it long before we learn to write it or <i>get our endings right</i>. Why as adults do we do it backwards? We start out in Jr. High grammar class and work our way back down to a pre-K speaker's functionality years later? Surely there is a better way. I just don't know what it is….
<br />
<br />Which brings me to another thought: the real shame is that I hadn't already started to learn Slovak 5 years ago. What does that say about me, that I can't speak my wife's native language? (A: I'm <b>a</b> shit.) (Part of the deal with Bosnian is that it is close to Slovak and will help me when I attempt to learn Slovak for real in a year or so.) More so, what does that say about Miriam that her native language is so complex? (A: she's <b>the</b> shit.)
