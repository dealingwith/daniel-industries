<font color="#333366"><b>Rob and the Dancer</b>
<br />
<br />I remember the first time I saw the play. I knew I had to be in it. I knew I had to be a part of it. I knew that it was my destiny. Never again to be in the daily <a href="http://www.saeco.it/Eng/Cf_prof_man_ms85.asp">grind</a>.
<br />
<br />Literally. I worked at a coffee shop.
<br />
<br />When I first saw that play, my heart burned within my heart with such a fire that the flames got real high and singed my <a href="http://www.ultranet.com/~jkimball/BiologyPages/C/CNS.html#medulla">medulla oblongata</a>. It took a while for my brain to reconnect; it took a couple days for my ears to again hear the voice of reason over the screaming of my soul.
<br />
<br />I just knew that that is what I had to do with my life.</font>
