<a href="http://www.felbers.net/blogger.html">Fanatical Apathy</a>
<br />
<br /><i>The dinner-party question that most people have no problem with is always a real trauma for me:
<br />
<br />"So, what do you do?"
<br />
<br />Inevitably, a difficult negotiation process follows. Most people who ask that question want a nice, simple answer. That is why most careers have handy names like "architect," "corporate vice president," or "Supreme Overlord of the Xynok Nebula." But mine doesn't.
<br />
<br />So I hedge my bets, trying to feel out the interest level of my inquisitor... "Uh, lots of things, um, you know, acting, writing, sort of..." I always feel really lame at this point. If they nod and smile and change the subject, I want to yell, "How can you leave it at that? You don't have the foggiest idea of what I do, you smug, self-important architect/ vice president/ Overlord!"
<br />
<br />But if they're actually interested, it's even worse. I have to try to tell them...
<br /></i>
