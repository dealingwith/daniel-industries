Angie is not an addict. She can quit anytime.
<br />
<br />Angie lives sometimes in a deserted house in Arlington. Sometimes at her friend Rodger’s house. Rodger isn’t an addict either. He works for the government and has a nice flat in Arlington.
<br />
<br />When Angie spends the night at the house, she wakes up to the birds chirping, across the street a huge building made out of the glass her window lacks. She climbs out of said window and scavenges a little behind the various food establishments on Wilson.
<br />
<br />When Angie spends the night at the flat, she steps out onto the balcony, smokes them if she’s got them, and wonders about all the people with normal lives across the street in the next high-rise building of flats.
<br />
<br />Angie is not an addict. She’s not that stinky all the time.
